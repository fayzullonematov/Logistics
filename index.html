About the Major

Logistics is the backbone of global trade and industry. It teaches students how to plan, organize, and control the movement of goods, services, and information from origin to destination. The program blends economics, business management, data analysis, and operations research to develop professionals who can optimize complex supply chains.

In university, students explore both the analytical and strategic dimensions of logistics, learning to design efficient networks, reduce costs, and enhance sustainability in transportation and distribution systems.

History of Logistics

The term “logistics” comes from the Greek word logistikos, meaning skilled in calculating. Its roots trace back to ancient military operations where organizing troops, weapons, and supplies was crucial for victory.

In the 20th century, logistics evolved beyond warfare into business and technology. With the rise of globalization, digitalization, and e-commerce, logistics became one of the most influential forces shaping how modern societies function.

Types of Logistics

Inbound Logistics

Focuses on the transportation and storage of raw materials and components coming into a company’s production process. It ensures a seamless supply of materials from suppliers to manufacturers.

Outbound Logistics

Deals with the movement of finished products from a company to the customer. It covers warehousing, packaging, distribution, and delivery.

Reverse Logistics

Manages the flow of returned goods, recycling, and waste disposal. It’s a critical part of sustainability and circular economy initiatives.

Third-Party Logistics (3PL)

Involves outsourcing logistics operations to specialized providers. These partners handle transportation, warehousing, and distribution so companies can focus on core activities.

Global Logistics

Centers on the management of international trade routes, customs, and cross-border regulations to maintain the global supply chain.

Green Logistics

Prioritizes environmentally friendly solutions — minimizing carbon footprints, using renewable energy, and promoting sustainable practices.
